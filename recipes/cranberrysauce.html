Cranberry Sauce

Description

This cranberry sauce recipe is the perfect blend of sweet and tart flavors, ideal for holiday dinners. Made with fresh cranberries and a touch of orange zest, it pairs beautifully with turkey, ham, and other savory dishes. Simple and quick to prepare, this sauce will bring a burst of color and flavor to your table.

Ingredients

  • Cranberries: Fresh cranberries are essential for this sauce, but frozen can work in a pinch.
  • Sugar: Granulated sugar adds sweetness to balance the tartness of the cranberries.
  • Water: Water helps create a sauce consistency and cooks the cranberries.
  • Orange zest: Adds a subtle citrus flavor that complements the cranberries.
  • Cinnamon: Optional but adds a warm, spiced flavor.

Steps

  1. Rinse the cranberries under cold water and discard any that are soft or damaged.
  2. Combine water and sugar in a medium saucepan and bring to a boil over medium heat.
  3. Add the cranberries, orange zest, and cinnamon (if using) to the saucepan.
  4. Reduce the heat and let the mixture simmer, stirring occasionally, until the cranberries burst and the sauce thickens (about 10-15 minutes).
  5. Remove from heat and let cool. The sauce will thicken as it cools.
  6. Transfer to a serving dish and refrigerate until ready to serve.
